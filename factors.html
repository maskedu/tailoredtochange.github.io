Psychological factors of purchasing














There are maybe a few psychological factors behind purchasing fast fashion (perhaps that cheap price tag?). But let me help you overcome and navigate through it so you can upgrade your sustainable footprint!




Shopping with a friend could perhaps stop that impulsive buying when you see that cheap price tag. Whenever you think about going shopping, get someone to stop you from purchasing that item from H&M or better yet, hopefully, they’ll stop you from visiting those fast fashion stores. This will be especially beneficial if they are a sustainable geek or if they just don’t shop at all! Hanging with a friend is always a good choice!

Instead of purchasing clothes, perhaps you could steal some off of your friend. This will reduce your spendings on new clothes and keep the environment clean!

You might want to set a goal in mind so that you stop your habits of impulsive buying. This could be something like only purchasing one new clothing garment every 4 months or when necessary. Better yet, purchase from a sustainable store.

Getting rid of those emails from that fast fashion store you regularly is a step-up to improving the temptation to click and online shop. You might also want to find a way so that you aren’t able to swipe your card or ask your parents for money to shop.

Every time you shop and look at the price tag, perhaps that’s a good time to remind yourself of why the price is so cheap and what about the people who are behind you, producing the product that’s in front. How were the workers paid? What conditions did they work under? These are all sorts of questions you can ask yourself.
