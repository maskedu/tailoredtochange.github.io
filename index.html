What is fast fashion?

Image on Flickr taken by Dana L. Brown on April 13, 2019

Fast fashion is a global part of the fashion industry. It is defined as the process of mass-producing garments that are of poor quality and easily disposable. Businesses use this model to manufacture their clothing at cheap costs (mostly done in third world countries) and use this as a method of exploitation of workers and resources. It is a rapid design that constantly produces new clothes that are in trend but of course, at an expense for the workers and the environment.
